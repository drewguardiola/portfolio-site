# Voice and tone

How copy reads on this site.

## In one line

Confident, technical, concise. Active verbs, quantified impact, no buzzwords. Read like an engineer wrote it, not a marketing department.

## Rules

1. **Lead with the outcome.** Every project card opens with a quantified impact line. `Increased lift/drag ratio from 1.89 → 2.03` beats `Worked on aerodynamic optimization`.
2. **Active voice, present-perfect or past.** `Designed`, `built`, `verified`, `coordinated`. Never `was responsible for`, `helped with`, `participated in`.
3. **Specific tools, not categories.** `ANSYS Fluent` not `simulation software`. `Siemens NX` not `CAD`. `DOORS Report Builder` not `requirements tooling`.
4. **Numbers earn the sentence.** If you don't have a number, mark the spot `[PLACEHOLDER]` rather than padding with adjectives.
5. **No buzzwords.** Avoid: synergy, leverage, robust, scalable, cutting-edge, passionate, journey, ecosystem, paradigm, holistic.
6. **No filler.** Cut every word that wouldn't survive a recruiter's 30-second skim.
7. **Tight sentence length.** Vary, but average ~15 words. Stack short sentences on each other for rhythm.

## Examples

**Bad:** "Worked on the aerodynamics subteam to help optimize the rear wing for better performance using industry-standard CFD tools."
**Good:** "Optimized the FSAE rear wing through iterative CFD. Raised lift/drag from 1.89 to 2.03."

**Bad:** "Passionate about aerospace and looking forward to leveraging my skills."
**Good:** "Mechanical engineering student. Aerospace systems, hardware, and CFD."

**Bad:** "Helped design baby pants in a team setting."
**Good:** "Led a 4-person team designing baby pants that secure children in high chairs for an external client."

## What to write where

- **Hero tagline**: one sentence. Period.
- **Project `summary`**: one sentence, ~20 words. What it is.
- **Project `impact`**: one sentence, opens with a verb and a number.
- **Project section bodies**: short paragraphs (2–4 sentences). Big visuals do the heavy lifting.
- **About bio**: 2–3 paragraphs. First-person OK but restrained. Lead with the engineering identity, not biography.

## Drew's voice specifically

- Aerospace direction is explicit. Internships, FSAE aero, CFD work — all point the same direction.
- Dual-degree (B.S./M.S.) is a credential, mention once on home + Resume, don't repeat.
- Honeywell experience is the strongest single signal. Surface it on home stats + Resume.
- Hobbies on About: human signal, not a personality test. One row, one word each.

## Things never to write

- "I'm passionate about..."
- "I love to learn..."
- "Welcome to my portfolio"
- "Thanks for visiting"
- Any sentence ending in an exclamation point
